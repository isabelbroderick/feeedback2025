Bias: When news is presented in a way that favors one side or viewpoint over another.

Unfinished Article

In news reporting, bias often falls into three general categories:

Left‐leaning bias: Stories that favor progressive or liberal viewpoints. You’ll see language that emphasizes social equality, government intervention, or critiques of traditional power structures..

Centrist-leaning bias: Articles that sit in the middle of the political spectrum—favoring neither extreme left nor extreme right. That doesn’t mean it’s free of bias; it simply means it favors moderate, middle ground policies rather than overally progressive or conservative policys .

Right‐leaning bias: Stories that favor conservative or traditional viewpoints. You’ll notice language celebrating free markets, individual responsibility, and skepticism of large‐scale government programs.
